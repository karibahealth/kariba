FAQ

We do get a lot of questions about our clinic and the care we provide. We hope this list of the most common is helpful

Is Kariba Health taking new patients?
Kariba Health is currently at capacity for new patients. We anticipate adding more capacity but at this time we are full. We believe in keeping our numbers a little lower to provide the best care to each patient that we can.
What if I need something requiring in-person care?
All in-person appointments must be preceded by a virtual consultation. This is for our safety and yours. We will also send you a covid screening questionaire prior to your visit. Masks are of course manditory and only the patient (or patient and one guardian) can be accommodated.
"Do you have my Results from x?"
Our front of house staff are not permitted to discuss results over the phone. This includes results from Specialists. If we receive results which are urgent, we will call you to discuss as soon as possible. You can also register for MyCareCompass to check your own results
"When will my doctor call?"
Please be ready for your call 10 mins before and up to 20 mins after the time given. Having the doctor run late is frustrating, so we do our best to allocate the correct amount of time to each booking. As this is just a best guess, however, we might run a little ahead or behind.
Lifelabs
All lifelab requesitions are sent directly to their central intake. You can simply visit your nearest lifelabs with your care-card and they will have the tests on file.
"I missed my appointment. Can I call back?"
All of our appointments are booked for a specific timeslot during the day, with no overlap. This means that if you miss your appointment you will need to make a new appointment. Please see our Cancellation Policy below
"What happens when you are unavailable?"
We understand that sometimes you can’t wait to be seen. On those occasions we recommend that you choose from one of the many urgent or emergency facilities available. The details of treatments or results will be sent to us so that we can continute to provide you with the very best longterm care.
Life Threatening
Do you think you are having a heart attack or stroke? Dial 911 immediately or have someone take you to your closest emergency room.
Urgent Mental Health Issue
We believe that a family doctor is a fantastic way to treat mental health concerns. There might be times, however, when we are unable to address a more urgent need and in those cases, we recommend the fantastic "Access and Assessment centre" at Vancouver General Hospital.
Other Urgent
Have you broken a leg, need stitches, having an asthma attack? Go to your local urgent care centre.

Accountability

As a team we work to be transparent, efficient and accountable. We ask the same from you as patients as you are an integral part of your health care team. Booking, results, follow up and referrals are all done through our online form. You book your own appointment and choose the reason for the visit at that time. We aim to offer you the best in modern health care.

Cancellation Policy

Every timeslot in the day is booked for one patient encounter with no overlap or double bookings. This helps keep us as on time as possible. As such we ask that you give us a minimum of 24 hours notice for cancellations. Failure to let us know (also known as a "no-show") wastes the slot which we could otherwise allocate to another patient. Our fee for no-show is the standard $50.
